The Feminist Tech Card Deck

Technology that is considered from a narrow or single perspective leads to unrepresentative regulation and the reinforcement of existing intersectional injustices. By analyzing tech through a feminist gaze, we begin to see the ways in which white, capitalist, patriarchal, and colonialist structures work together and influence tech processes. These principles look at tech through a critical lens, observing and addressing the power structures behind technology creation, regulation, and use, and offering a framework for creating otherwise.

The Feminist Tech Principles

The Feminist Tech principles are a work in progress set of guidelines for feminist tech policy-making and technology creation. They have been drafted by various people SUPERRR Lab has invited into the process; activists, policymakers, writers, designers, technologists, researchers, and educators, that advocate for digital rights and the rights of marginalized groups.

{% include partials/ftf-pill.liquid text='Global' href='/feministtech/principles/#global' classes="mb-3 mb-sm-0" %} {% include partials/ftf-pill.liquid text='Societal' href='/feministtech/principles/#societal' classes="mb-3 mb-sm-0" %} {% include partials/ftf-pill.liquid text='Interpersonal' href='/feministtech/principles/#interpersonal' classes="mb-3 mb-sm-0" %} {% include partials/ftf-pill.liquid text='The Self' href='/feministtech/principles/#the-self' classes="mb-3 mb-sm-0" %}

Each principle is supported by insights into the status quo, a brief overview on why we think the principle is necessary, and a good practice example of people or organizations who are already working on this topic in a feminist way. To make the topic more tangible and accessible to a wider audience, we turn to storytelling by developing a set of future narratives for some of the principles, with the aim to expand further.
